Bottom Line Up Front

Palo Alto Networks leads AIOps through Cortex XDR's cross-product event correlation and Strata Copilot's natural language policy authoring — the only vendor with a native SOAR product (XSOAR) deeply integrated with its SASE stack. Zscaler leads on DEM/RCA maturity (ZDX) — per-session diagnostic depth is unmatched for enterprise IT operations. Cato Networks delivers the best autonomous path optimization by virtue of owning the full network path, and in March 2026 closed the UEBA gap with Cato Dynamic Prevention — an auto-adaptive threat prevention engine correlating months of signals across all inline sensors. Cloudflare is the weakest AIOps vendor in the Big Five — no mature UEBA, no native SOAR, and no GenAI policy authoring as of Q2 2026. Netskope's AIOps is strong within its SSE domain (DLP + UEBA correlation) but trails on SD-WAN operations and network diagnostics.

AIOps in SASE: From "alert flood" to "tell me what to fix"

A SASE platform managing 50,000 users generates millions of log events per day. Without AI, that's a wall of noise that only a large, experienced security team can process. AIOps converts that noise into three actionable outputs: who did something risky (UEBA), why is the network slow (path diagnostics and RCA), and what should my policy look like (GenAI policy authoring).

The architectural advantage matters here. Vendors that own the full network path (Cato with its private backbone) can diagnose latency problems with complete hop visibility. Vendors relying on internet transit can only see their own portion — which is why ZDX is so important to Zscaler: it's the measurement layer that compensates for not owning the infrastructure.

GenAI policy authoring in 2026 is moving from "template wizard" to "tell me what you want in plain English and I'll write the rule." Palo Alto's Strata Copilot writes the rule, simulates its effect, and explains the change. This reduces the policy change cycle from 45 minutes of manual work to 3 minutes of review-and-approve — a direct operational impact metric.

Vendor Deep Dives

Palo Alto Networks — Cortex XDR + Strata Copilot + XSOAR

CORTEX PLATFORM + SCM COPILOT

Palo Alto's AIOps story is the most integrated in the Big Five. Cortex XDR provides behavioral analytics and cross-product correlation, pulling telemetry from Prisma Access, physical NGFWs, endpoints (via XDR agent), and cloud environments. Strata Copilot in SCM provides the GenAI operational layer: natural language policy management, AI-driven change recommendations, and configuration compliance analysis. XSOAR closes the loop by automating response actions triggered by Cortex detections.

UEBA + Cross-Product Correlation — Best-in-Class

Cortex XDR's UEBA performs ML-based behavioral baselining with peer-group comparison — a user's activity is measured against their own historical baseline AND peers in the same role. Multi-dimensional scoring accounts for time-of-day, location, application access, and data movement behavior simultaneously. When a UEBA anomaly fires, Cortex XDR automatically correlates it with Prisma Access network events and endpoint telemetry, producing a unified incident with MITRE ATT&CK mapping and an auto-generated narrative explanation. No other Big Five vendor produces correlated incidents of this quality across this many telemetry sources from a single platform.

GenAI Policy Authoring — Strata Copilot

Strata Copilot in SCM is the most mature natural language policy authoring tool in the Big Five as of Q1 2026. An administrator can describe a policy in plain English; Copilot writes the App-ID rule, shows a simulation of which current traffic it would affect, identifies potential unintended side effects, and explains the rule for audit purposes. The simulation-before-commit capability is the key differentiator — other vendors offer policy suggestions but not pre-commit traffic impact previews.

SIEM/SOAR — Native XSOAR Advantage

Cortex XSOAR is the only native SOAR product among the Big Five SASE vendors. When Cortex XDR fires a UEBA alert, XSOAR can automatically quarantine the endpoint, notify the user's manager, open an IT ticket, and send a Teams/Slack notification — all without a webhook or API middleware layer. For organizations already using XSOAR, this is a material operational advantage.

▲ Strengths

Best cross-product event correlation (Prisma Access + NGFW + endpoint). Strata Copilot natural language policy with simulation preview. Native XSOAR SOAR integration. WildFire telemetry feeds UEBA with threat context. Best for organizations with existing Palo Alto + Cortex footprint.

▼ Watch Areas

Requires Cortex XDR license for full UEBA — not included in base Prisma Access. Full AIOps story spans 3+ products (SCM + Cortex + XSOAR). Path optimization is advisory for ISP-managed WAN segments. Licensing complexity.

Zscaler — UEBA + ZDX + AI Admin

ZDX DIAGNOSTICS + UEBA + AI ADMIN LAYER
Research Note: Zscaler's AI/AIOps features span ZIA, ZPA, and ZDX — three separate products. Feature availability varies by license bundle. Verify current scope before positioning.

Zscaler's AIOps centers on two strengths: ZDX for the best network diagnostics in the market, and UEBA for ML-based behavioral anomaly detection that integrates identity risk scores with network access patterns. Natural language log queries and policy recommendations are available, but full natural language-to-committed-policy with simulation is still roadmap as of Q1 2026.

UEBA — Deep Identity + Network Integration

Zscaler UEBA correlates ZIA (web/SaaS access), ZPA (private app access), and ZDX (network path telemetry) into per-user risk scores. A user accessing an unusual private app at an unusual hour from an unusual location with degraded ZDX scores generates a compound risk signal that single-source UEBA cannot produce. Automated risk escalation adjusts ZPA access policy dynamically — a high-risk user can be stepped down to read-only or blocked from sensitive app segments without a human in the loop.

Root Cause Analysis (ZDX) — Best-in-Class

ZDX's RCA capability is the best in the Big Five for enterprise IT operations. When a user reports "my app is slow," ZDX provides per-hop path latency, identification of whether the degradation is in "device," "ISP," "Zscaler cloud," or "application," comparison against historical baseline and peer-group baseline, and a written diagnosis summary for helpdesk staff. Target diagnosis time is under 60 seconds from ticket open to fault identification — a documented capability that significantly reduces MTTR for remote worker issues.

▲ Strengths

Best RCA/DEM via ZDX — fastest fault identification in the market. Strong UEBA with multi-source risk scoring. UEBA risk scores feed ZPA policy dynamically. Large enterprise installed base = most validated AIOps at scale.

▼ Watch Areas

ZDX is separately licensed — include in TCO. GenAI policy authoring not GA (Q1 2026). Full AIOps requires ZIA + ZPA + ZDX licenses. No native SOAR product.

Cato Networks — Cato XDR + AI Assistant + Autonomous Backbone

SINGLE-VENDOR PLATFORM + PRIVATE BACKBONE ADVANTAGE

Cato's AIOps advantage is structural: owning the complete network path means Cato can diagnose latency problems with complete hop visibility that ISP-dependent vendors cannot replicate. Cato XDR extends this into security operations, correlating network telemetry, endpoint data (if Cato EDR deployed), and SASE events in a single platform.

Autonomous Path Optimization — Structural Advantage

Because Cato controls both the edge appliance (Socket) and the backbone between PoPs, path optimization can be genuinely predictive. Cato's backbone telemetry is collected every second at every PoP — latency trends, packet loss, jitter — and the path steering algorithm reroutes traffic before a SLA breach is detectable by the user. Competing vendors performing path optimization on customer-ISP links can only react to observed degradation; Cato can act 30–90 seconds ahead of user-visible impact.

UEBA + Dynamic Prevention + AI Assistant

Cato XDR includes UEBA with ML-based baselining, and cross-product correlation within the Cato platform is seamless — unlike PA (Cortex is separate) or Zscaler (ZDX is separate), Cato XDR's UEBA and network telemetry share the same data store by design. In March 2026, Cato launched Dynamic Prevention — an auto-adaptive threat prevention engine that continuously correlates months of activity across inline sensors (DLP, IPS, NGAM) and out-of-band engines to detect behavior-based threats that appear benign in isolation. Once identified, Cato automatically applies adaptive restrictions without SOC intervention. This closes the historical UEBA depth gap against Palo Alto and Zscaler. The AI Assistant in CMA supports natural language queries for policy status and anomaly explanations; full NL-to-committed-policy with simulation is in active development but not fully GA as of Q2 2026.

▲ Strengths

Structural path optimization advantage from private backbone ownership. Single-platform XDR — no product integration required. Cato XDR is included, not a separate license. Dynamic Prevention (March 2026) adds auto-adaptive behavioral blocking without SOC intervention. Best AIOps story for Lean IT teams that still need mature UEBA.

▼ Watch Areas

Endpoint UEBA requires Cato EDR — cross-EDR correlation needs SIEM. AI Assistant NL-to-policy authoring not yet GA. Breadth of cross-product correlation narrower than PA Cortex. SIEM/SOAR integration depth trails PA. Dynamic Prevention is new (March 2026) — evaluate detection accuracy and false-positive rates in PoC.

Netskope — UEBA + DLP-Informed Analytics

DATA-CENTRIC BEHAVIORAL ANALYTICS

Netskope's AIOps is strongest at the intersection of UEBA and DLP — a user's risk score is informed by both network behavior AND data access patterns. A user who accesses unusually sensitive data in an unusual app at an unusual time generates a compound risk signal that connects network behavior to data risk. This is architecturally different from pure network-behavior UEBA and directly serves regulated industry insider threat use cases.

UEBA + DLP Correlation

Netskope UEBA baselines user behavior across SaaS, web, and private app access, then cross-references behavioral anomalies with DLP events — when a user's access pattern changes AND they trigger a DLP event (sensitive file download), the compound risk score is significantly elevated beyond what either signal alone would produce. Automated policy response can restrict the user's access scope mid-session.

▲ Strengths

Best DLP + UEBA correlation for insider threat detection. Data-movement-aware behavioral analytics unique to Netskope. Strong SIEM integration (Splunk, Sentinel, QRadar with bidirectional enrichment). Best AIOps for regulated industry insider threat programs.

▼ Watch Areas

GenAI policy authoring not yet GA. No SD-WAN path optimization story. No native SOAR. Cross-product correlation with endpoint and branch WAN requires SIEM. RCA for network path issues trails Zscaler ZDX significantly.

Cloudflare — Cloudflare One Analytics + DEX

EDGE ANALYTICS + DEVELOPING CAPABILITIES

Cloudflare is the weakest AIOps vendor in the Big Five — not a close call. No mature UEBA, no native SOAR, no GenAI policy authoring, and DEM (Cloudflare DEX) that is functional but less mature than Zscaler ZDX or Palo Alto ADEM. Cloudflare's AIOps strength is its raw telemetry depth from internet-scale traffic, but this advantage is not yet translated into enterprise-ready AIOps products as of Q1 2026.

DEM (DEX) and UEBA Status

Cloudflare DEX provides per-device, per-application latency metrics, synthetic monitoring probes, and basic path tracing. ISP fault attribution and hop-level analysis are in active development. For enterprise IT operations teams needing sub-60-second automated fault attribution, ZDX is materially better. Cloudflare does not offer a mature UEBA product as of Q1 2026 — structured ML-based behavioral baselining with multi-dimensional scoring and automated risk escalation is not a current product capability.

▲ Strengths

Internet-scale passive DNS and traffic telemetry feeds threat intelligence with unique breadth. DEX is improving rapidly — strong trajectory. Fastest SASE platform for deploying policy changes (API-first, Terraform-native). Best for developer-centric ops teams.

▼ Watch Areas

No mature UEBA. No GenAI policy authoring. DEX lags ZDX for enterprise diagnostics. No native SOAR. Event correlation is log aggregation, not ML-driven incident grouping. Not recommended as primary SASE for enterprise security ops teams with active UEBA requirements in 2026.

AIOps in 2026: Three Defining Shifts

1. Natural language policy authoring is graduating from demo to production. Palo Alto's Strata Copilot with simulation preview is in production use at enterprise scale. Cato's AI Assistant and Zscaler's AI admin features are approaching GA. By end of 2026, NL policy authoring with pre-commit traffic impact simulation will be table stakes — vendors that don't ship it will lose administrator workflow deals.

2. UEBA is moving from "user behavior" to "user + data behavior." Netskope's model — correlating network behavior with data sensitivity signals — is the right architecture for 2026 insider threat detection. Expect Palo Alto (via Cortex + Enterprise DLP integration) and Zscaler (via ZIA DLP + UEBA correlation deepening) to close this gap by late 2026.

3. The "Middle Mile" latency problem is finally being solved autonomously. Cato's private backbone path optimization — predictive rerouting ahead of SLA breach — is the model that enterprises with global branch footprints should evaluate. The alternative (ISP-managed MPLS + reactive SD-WAN steering) is being replaced by predictive backbone management that operates without human intervention.
