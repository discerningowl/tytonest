Kettlebell Program

Tyto Alba • Starting March 8, 2026 • 30–35 min KB → 30 min cardio

Program Overview

Progression follows two levers: reps first, then weight. Fill the top of the rep range across all rounds before moving up. The Turkish Get-Up is form-gated — never load-gated.

Weight ladder: 20# → 35# → 45#

Key Principles

  • Reps before weight — fill the rep range across all rounds before increasing the bell
  • Two-week rule — only increase one variable every two weeks maximum
  • Form first — if form breaks down, hold the current load for another block
  • Post-lunch timing — peak lisinopril effect aligns with your window; stay well hydrated
  • Rest 30–45 sec between exercises to maintain cardiovascular demand

Progression Notes

Wks 1–4 · Foundation

Build movement patterns. Stay at the lower end of rep ranges. Focus on form over speed. Lisinopril is establishing full efficacy — monitor for dizziness post-set.

Wks 5–8 · Build

Reps increase across all exercises. Circuits should feel harder. BP should be stabilizing — a great time for a doctor check-in.

Wks 9–12 · Progress

Add a 4th round across all days. Meaningful volume jump — expect some fatigue in week 9. Stay consistent with hydration.

Wks 13–16 · Challenge

First weight increases on primary moves (35# → 45#). Drop to 3 rounds if needed when moving up. Visceral fat loss typically accelerates here.

Wks 17–20 · Peak

Highest volume and load of the program. Consider a doctor reassessment — BP medication review may be appropriate given weight loss progress.

Weight jump note: If 45# feels too large a jump on any exercise, stay at 35# for one additional block. The Turkish Get-Up weight increase is always form-gated — never load-gated.
